Instruction

In this HIT, you will work with charts (similar to the charts in Excel). The HIT interface is outlined in the following figure. It contains 3 main blocks: a Reference Chart (block A in the figure), A Collection of charts (block C in the figure) and A Similarity block (block B in the figure) with five squares. Each of the squares in block B is assigned a number from 5 to 1.

Your task will be to compare each of the charts in the Collection (block C) with the Reference Chart (block A) and measure how similar they are to the reference. Then you should drag the charts from the Collection (block C) to one of the squares in Similarity block (block B) according to their similarity to the Reference Chart. The numbers of squares stand for different similarity levels to the Reference Chart (5 for most similar and 1 for least similar).

Note that, the perception of similarity is completely up to you.

Step by step instructions:

Please read the following instructions and then click OK to begin the task:

  1. Given the Reference chart and Collection charts, drag the charts in Collection block to any of the squares in Similarity block based on their similarity to the reference (1 for least similar; 5 for most similar). The charts in the Collection block and the Similarity block are magnified if you hover over them.

  2. You MUST assign ALL the charts in the Collection block to the squares in the Similarity block you seem appropriate. You can also move a chart from one square to another if required.

  3. After the assignment is complete, click the Freeze button to freeze the task. You will NOT be able to make any changes to the Similarity block once the task is frozen. But you can still see the larger charts by hovering on the charts in the bins.

  4. After you click Freeze, several questions related to the rating task will appear. Once you answer all the questions, you can click Submit to finish the task.

  5. Whenever you want to see the instructions during your assignment, you can click the Instruction tab at the top. You can click it again to hide it.
